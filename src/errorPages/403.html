What you can do

Your local IT admin team should be able to support you, so contact them first if possible.

If you cannot contact them right now, there are some things you can try yourself.

Clearing your cookies often resolves the problem so do this first.

Clear your cookies

Use this button to clear your cookies and go back to the start page. You should then be able to access the SR1 service.

If you get this error message again, try another suggestion on this page.

Use a different browser

If you’re using Microsoft Edge, try accessing the SR1 service using Google Chrome. To open Chrome, go to your device's start menu and look for the Chrome logo.

Check your role is authorised

Only these job roles are authorised to complete an SR1 form:

  • GP
  • hospital consultant
  • specialty doctor
  • hospice doctor
  • senior specialist nurse (such as a clinical nurse specialist, advanced nurse practitioner or similar)

Your job role is checked against the job role on your NHS smartcard. If you should have access to this service, make sure your smartcard is correct. If it’s wrong, your IT support team will be able to help.

If you do not have an account to log incidents with the NHS National Service Desk call 0300 303 5035.

If you still cannot log on to the SR1 service

You can complete PDF versions of the SR1 and the fee claim forms online. They do not need to be signed, but you’ll need to return them to us from a secure email address. Any email that does not come from a secure email address will be automatically deleted.

Secure email addresses end in:

  • @nhs.net
  • @nhs.uk
  • @nhs.scot
  • @nhs.wales.uk
  • @gov.uk

Send the request for a PDF version of the SR1 form, and fee claim form if needed, from your secure email address to form.e-sr1@dwp.gov.uk. Please include your:

Please include your:

  • surgery/hospital/postal address
  • phone number

Do not encrypt any part of this email.
